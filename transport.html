Getting Around

Japan boasts an extensive and efficient transportation system, making it easy for travelers to explore cities and regions. Here are the main options:

1. Trains

The train network in Japan is renowned for its punctuality and cleanliness. The Shinkansen (bullet train) is a must-try for fast travel between major cities.

2. Subways

Subway systems in cities like Tokyo and Osaka are convenient for navigating urban areas. Purchase a prepaid Suica or Pasmo card for easy access.

3. Buses

Buses are an excellent option for reaching places not serviced by trains. Look for local bus routes, and consider using apps to find schedules.

4. Taxis

Taxis are widely available but can be expensive. Ensure you have your destination written in Japanese to show the driver.

Tips for Using Public Transport

  • Always be on time; trains leave promptly.
  • Keep noise to a minimum; it's considered rude to talk loudly in public transport.
  • Many train stations have English signage, but learning a few basic Japanese phrases can be helpful.
